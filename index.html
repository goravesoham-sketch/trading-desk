A progressive path from foundation to mastery — each stage has a clear outcome and a benchmark you check yourself against, using the tools in this desk. Check off benchmarks as you genuinely meet them; ticking a box doesn't create the skill, the practice behind it does.

Stage 1 — Foundation

Beginner
Outcome: you can read a chart's structure and size a position correctly, every time, without needing to think about the math.
Core principles
  • Market structure: HH, HL, LH, LL and what defines an uptrend vs downtrend
  • Support and resistance as buy-zone / sell-zone, not exact lines
  • Candlestick basics: Hammer, Engulfing, Doji and what each implies
  • Risk math: lot size = (account × risk%) ÷ (stop pips × pip value)
Benchmarks
Can mark HH/HL/LH/LL correctly on 10 historical XAUUSD charts without help
Can calculate correct lot size for 5 different account/risk/stop combinations using the Position Calculator, unaided
Has never placed a demo/live trade above 1% risk

Stage 2 — Development

Intermediate
Outcome: you can apply SMC concepts to find and confirm setups, not just recognize them on a chart someone else marked up.
Core principles
  • Liquidity: external (30m/1H) vs internal (5m/15m), and why sweeps happen
  • FVG / imbalance identification and validity rules
  • BOS vs CHoCH, and market structure shift (MSS) on the entry timeframe
  • Trendline + 9 EMA setup for entry timing
Benchmarks
Has logged at least 20 backtests in the Backtesting Log with a setup tag on each
Can point to a liquidity sweep and the FVG it created on a live chart within 60 seconds
Waits for BOS/CHoCH confirmation before entry on every logged trade

Stage 3 — Proficiency

Advanced
Outcome: consistent execution under real conditions — discipline holds even when a trade goes against you or the week goes badly.
Core principles
  • Session timing: London/NY overlap vs thin Asian-session liquidity
  • News-day discipline: knowing when NOT to trade, not just when to
  • R:R discipline — target 1:1.5 minimum, sized to the stop, not the reverse
  • Recognizing your own revenge-trading or overconfidence patterns
Benchmarks
30 consecutive days logged in the Journal with zero risk-rule breaks
Has run the AI Psychology Check at least twice with no unresolved red flags
Sat out at least one high-impact news day on purpose, logged the reason why

Stage 4 — Mastery

Expert
Outcome: a verified, data-backed edge — you know which setups actually work for you, not which ones feel right.
Core principles
  • Multi-timeframe confluence: aligning SMC structure with Gann cycle dates
  • Edge verification: win-rate and expectancy by setup tag, not overall
  • Capital scaling: moving from demo/small account to a funded evaluation
  • Reviewing your own trades as critically as you'd review a stranger's
Benchmarks
Has 50+ logged trades and can name the single best-performing setup tag with data to back it
Passed (or actively attempting) a funded account evaluation with documented rules compliance
3+ months of positive expectancy, verified from the Journal's own numbers, not memory

Candlestick Patterns
Hammer Buy zone
Small body near the top, long lower wick. Body should be roughly 2–3x weaker (smaller) than the wick. Look for it sitting right at a support / buy zone.
Inverted Hammer Sell zone
Small body near the bottom, long upper wick. A powerful reversal signal at resistance — body 2–3x weaker than the wick.
Bullish Engulfing Buy zone
Small red candle followed by a big green candle that fully engulfs it. Marks a downtrend-to-reversal shift at a buy zone. Best read on 15m/30m timeframe.
Bearish Engulfing Sell zone
Small green candle followed by a big red candle that fully engulfs it, right at resistance/sell zone. 15m/30m timeframe.
Hanging Man Sell zone
Looks like a hammer but appears at resistance / top of a move. Needs confirmation — check for selling pressure before acting.
Doji
Open and close are almost equal — a tug-of-war candle. Signals indecision; watch the next candle for direction, don't act on the doji alone.
Morning Star Buy zone
Three-candle bullish reversal: big red candle, small indecisive candle, then a big green candle closing well into the first candle's body. Marks exhaustion of a downtrend.
Evening Star Sell zone
Three-candle bearish reversal: big green candle, small indecisive candle, then a big red candle closing well into the first candle's body. Marks exhaustion of an uptrend.
Three White Soldiers Buy zone
Three consecutive solid green candles, each closing higher than the last. Strong continuation/reversal-confirmation signal — momentum is clearly with buyers.
Three Black Crows Sell zone
Three consecutive solid red candles, each closing lower than the last. Strong bearish momentum signal — mirror image of Three White Soldiers.
Chart Patterns — Reversal
Head & Shoulders Sell zone
Left shoulder, higher head, right shoulder, all resting on a neckline. Break of the neckline after the right shoulder signals a trend reversal from up to down.
Inverted Head & Shoulders Buy zone
Mirror image of Head & Shoulders — left shoulder, lower head, right shoulder. Neckline break upward signals a reversal from down to up.
Double Top Sell zone
Two roughly equal highs with a pullback between them. Break below the support/neckline confirms a bearish reversal.
Double Bottom Buy zone
Two roughly equal lows with a bounce between them. Break above the resistance/neckline confirms a bullish reversal.
Triple Top Sell zone
Three roughly equal highs testing the same resistance. Failure to break through, followed by a support break, signals a stronger reversal than a double top.
Triple Bottom Buy zone
Three roughly equal lows testing the same support. Breakout above resistance after the third test signals a strong bullish reversal.
Rising Wedge Sell zone
Price grinds higher inside two converging upward lines. Despite the uptrend appearance, it usually resolves with a bearish breakdown — momentum is quietly fading.
Falling Wedge Buy zone
Price grinds lower inside two converging downward lines. Despite the downtrend appearance, it usually resolves with a bullish breakout.
Bullish Diamond Buy zone
Rare pattern that widens then narrows into a diamond shape, often resembling an inverted head & shoulders. Breakout above the diamond signals continuation or reversal higher.
Bearish Diamond Sell zone
Same diamond shape appearing after an uptrend. Breakdown below the diamond signals a reversal from bullish to bearish.
Chart Patterns — Continuation
Bull Flag Continuation
A strong upward move (flagpole) followed by a small downward-sloping consolidation channel. Breakout above the channel continues the original uptrend. Common and reliable.
Bear Flag Continuation
A strong downward move (flagpole) followed by a small upward-sloping consolidation channel. Breakdown below the channel continues the original downtrend.
Ascending Triangle Continuation
Flat resistance on top, rising support below. Buyers are stepping in more aggressively each time — breakout above resistance is the usual (bullish) resolution.
Descending Triangle Continuation
Flat support below, falling resistance above. Sellers are stepping in more aggressively each time — breakdown below support is the usual (bearish) resolution.
Symmetrical Triangle
Converging support and resistance lines, both sloping toward each other. Direction-neutral — trades in whichever direction it breaks, usually with the prior trend.
Bullish / Bearish Pennant
Like a flag but the consolidation converges into a small symmetrical triangle instead of a channel. Same idea — brief pause before the prior move continues.
Bullish / Bearish Rectangle
Price bounces between flat, parallel support and resistance (a trading range). Breakout direction, up or down, usually continues the trend that preceded the range.
Structure & Entry Setup
Support / Resistance
Support = zone where buyers activate (buy zone). Resistance = zone where sellers activate (sell zone). Never trade blind breakouts — wait for confirmation before buying/selling through a level.
Trendline + 9 EMA Setup
Draw trendline on 4H/1H once trend is identified. Support trendline → buy side only. Resistance trendline → sell side only. Entry confirmation: 5-min candle closes with weak body beyond the 9 EMA. Use 30m/15m for the trendline touch, 5m for entry timing.
Market Structure (HH/HL/LH/LL)
Uptrend: Higher Highs + Higher Lows — don't sell into pullbacks, buy them. Downtrend: Lower Highs + Lower Lows — don't buy into pullbacks, sell them. Trend only changes when a HL (uptrend) or LH (downtrend) breaks, with 40–50% retracement confirming it.
SMC Glossary
BOS Break of Structure
Price makes a new high/low in the same direction as the trend — trend continuation confirmation.
CHoCH Change of Character
First sign the trend itself may be reversing — opposite of BOS.
OB Order Block
The last candle before a strong displacement move — where institutional orders likely sit.
FVG Fair Value Gap
A 3-candle gap left by a sudden move. Valid only if price hasn't reacted to it yet. Buy FVG = gap left by a green candle; Sell FVG = gap left by a red candle.
Liquidity External / Internal
Pools sit where the market's last strong buying/selling happened. External liquidity = 30m/1H zones. Internal liquidity = 5m/15m zones. Price often sweeps liquidity before reversing.
MSS Market Structure Shift
After a liquidity sweep, watch the 5m timeframe for a swing high/low break — that's your entry trigger signal to look for, not act on blindly.
Displacement
A strong, decisive candle that moves clean through a zone with force — usually what creates a fresh FVG/imbalance.

Candle Closing & Weak Body
Candle ची closing खूप महत्वाची आहे — उदा. 30min. Hammer/Inverted Hammer साठी candle ची Body पेक्षा weak (wick) 2/3 पट जास्त असावी.
Trendline Mechanics
Support vs Resistance Trendline
Support trendline → Buying साठी वापरली जाते, powerful trendline आहे. Resistance trendline → downtrend मध्ये, Sell side चा trade घेण्यासाठी उपयोगी. Draw 4Hr वर, mark करताना 2 किंवा त्यापेक्षा जास्त point पाहिजेत.
Trendline Brake & Retest
Trendline brake झाल्यावर आधी slow होऊन brake होणे, नंतर Retest करून down/up जातो — तेव्हाच confirm मानावे, पहिल्याच touch वर trade घेऊ नये.
9 EMA Confirmation
Support trendline वर entry plan झाल्यानंतर market 9EMA च्या वरती sustain होतंय का ते 5min ला बघणे — confirmation साठी. Buy plan मध्ये 9EMA च्या वरती candle Body weak sustain होणे पाहिजे.
Structure Mapping & Liquidity Mechanics
40%/50% Retracement Rule
BOS सोबत मागच्या HL लक्षात ठेवावा — LH/HL चा 40% किंवा 50% Retracement झाल्यावरच trend change खरा मानावा. फक्त HL/LH brake होणे पुरेसे नाही, retracement सोबत असावा.
Buy FVG vs Sell FVG
FVG चे 2 प्रकार: Buy FVG → Buying trade साठी, green color candle पासून 3 candle च्या मध्ये gap. Sell FVG → Selling trade साठी, Red 3 candle पासून gap. रोज सकाळी FVG मार्क करणे, नंतर trendline mark करणे — FVG तेव्हाच valid जो market अजून react झाला नाही.
External vs Internal Liquidity
External liquidity → 30min/1Hr चा FVG/zone. Internal liquidity → 15min/5min चा zone. Liquidity bank म्हणजे "Last time strong Buying/strong selling" झालेली जागा (30min).
Liquidity Sweep नंतर Entry
Entry plan साठी 5min TF वापरायची. Liquidity sweep नंतर Buy असेल तर swing high mark करणे / Sell असेल तर swing low mark करायचे. Market Structure Shift (MSS) — swing high/low break — याची वाट बघणे, मगच entry.
